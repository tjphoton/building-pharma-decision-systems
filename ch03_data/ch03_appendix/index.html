    Appendix 3A: Field Reference

    Nothing below needs to be read linearly. Each table maps a source, record type, or field group to its analytical uses and main caution.

    Table 3A.1. Common data sources for pharmaceutical decision science.

    Data source Common vendor sources Internal sources Public or open alternatives Typical use
    Medical and pharmacy claims IQVIA, Komodo Health, Symphony Health Adjudicated claims received through company agreements CMS synthetic claims, CMS Part D Prescribers, Medicaid drug utilization Market sizing, treatment patterns, patient journeys, HCP opportunity
    EHR and clinical data IQVIA and licensed health-system feeds Clinical research or approved health-system data Synthea, OHDSI Eunomia, MIMIC-IV Demo Disease status, biomarkers, treatment context, cohort design
    CRM and field activity Veeva Vault CRM Calls, meetings, messages, territories, account plans None at customer level; this book uses synthetic interaction records Contact history, field execution, targeting, next best action
    HCP digital engagement Veeva, Doximity, Medscape Approved email, HCP web, webinar, and content-interaction records None at customer level; this book uses synthetic engagement events HCP engagement, content response, channel preference, omnichannel sequencing
    Consumer media and digital marketing Media agencies; TV and streaming measurement; Google Ads, Meta, TikTok Campaign plans, spend, impressions, reach, clicks, conversions Public ad libraries, search trends, synthetic campaign data Awareness, patient education, campaign measurement, media mix modeling
    Formulary and access Licensed formulary and payer-policy feeds Market-access research and contracting records CMS Part D formulary data Coverage, restrictions, access barriers, reachable opportunity
    Patient services and specialty pharmacy Hub and specialty-pharmacy vendors Patient-support program records None complete; this book uses synthetic case events Authorization, fulfillment, affordability, abandonment
    Provider and account reference IQVIA OneKey, Veeva OpenData Customer masters, affiliations, territories, hierarchies NPPES Specialty, location, affiliation, territory alignment
    Product and competitive reference Licensed product and market-intelligence feeds Product masters, competitive intelligence openFDA, ClinicalTrials.gov Product dictionaries, label context, competitive development
    Sales, finance, and supply IQVIA sales and prescription products ERP, shipments, chargebacks, inventory, finance CMS and Medicaid utilization aggregates as benchmarks Demand, revenue, inventory, forecast performance

    Table 3A.2. Claims components and their analytical uses.

    Record component What one record represents Important fields Later analytical uses Main caution
    Medical claim A billed encounter or service Patient, dates, diagnoses, procedures, HCP, account, payer, setting, status, amounts Disease prevalence, diagnosed populations, utilization, sites of care, provider volume, account opportunity, comorbidities, costs, market sizing One episode can create several claims; billing codes do not fully describe clinical intent
    Pharmacy transaction A submitted prescription transaction Patient, NDC, fill date, status, quantity, days supply, prescriber, payer, patient pay Treatment adoption, refills, persistence, switching, abandonment, rejection analysis, access friction, prescriber activity, market share Rejections and reversals are not completed fills; dispensing does not prove ingestion
    Coverage period A window when a patient is observable Patient, payer, start date, end date Denominators, eligibility, lookback periods, incidence cohorts, treatment gaps, persistence Loss of observation mimics treatment discontinuation
    Provider reference One HCP and attributes Specialty, taxonomy, location, account affiliation Provider matching, specialty segmentation, referral networks, targeting, alignment Affiliations, locations, and specialties go stale
    Account reference One healthcare organization Type, location, sales region, capacity Account opportunity, provider aggregation, call planning, workload design Account definitions vary by business purpose
    Payer reference One payer or plan Payer type, plan, geography Payer mix, access segmentation, utilization comparison Operational benefit designs need extra mappings
    Code dictionary One diagnosis, procedure, or product code Code, description, product, ingredient Disease definitions, product mapping, treatment baskets, quality checks Codes, mappings, and packages change over time

    Table 3A.3. Formulary fields and their analytical uses.

    Field group Important fields What it describes Later analytical uses Main caution
    Plan and product plan_id, plan_name, product_name The payer-product combination Payer comparison, product access, competitive positioning Plan IDs may need mapping to parent organizations
    Coverage level tier, prior_tier, new_tier Patient cost-sharing position Access scoring, tier movement, reachable opportunity Tier labels are not comparable across all plans
    Utilization management Prior-authorization and step-therapy fields Requirements before coverage Restriction burden, rejection analysis, support demand Published rules miss operational exceptions
    Specialty distribution specialty_pharmacy Whether dispensing is restricted to SP Fulfillment pathways, channel planning, abandonment Requirements vary by product and benefit
    Time Effective dates and quarter When a policy applies Historical joins, pre-post comparisons Current status must not be applied to earlier events
    Change classification change_type Whether tier or restrictions changed Event studies, access monitoring A change event alone proves no effect

    Table 3A.4. EHR record components and their analytical uses.

    EHR record component Important fields What it contributes Later analytical uses Main caution
    Encounter Patient, date, visit type, note type Clinical setting and timing Visit patterns, cohort entry, follow-up Documentation can be incomplete
    Problem list Active diagnosis codes Documented current conditions Comorbidity extraction, eligibility filters Often stale or incomplete
    Medication order Product, dose, route, frequency Documented treatment intent Prior/concomitant therapy, treatment context An order is not a dispense
    Laboratory result Test, value, unit, reference range, date Clinical measurement Biomarker cohorts, disease status, eligibility Interpretation depends on timing, units, and rules
    Abnormal flag H/L range indicator Crude comparison with reference range Screening, data-quality checks Never determines eligibility by itself

    Table 3A.5. CRM, field activity, and HCP digital engagement records.

    Record type Important fields What it describes Later analytical uses Main caution
    CRM interaction Rep, HCP, account, date, product, channel Recorded field activity Call patterns, reach, frequency, targeting, channel mix A logged activity does not prove effective contact
    Message and outcome message_topic, outcome, next_action Recorded content and follow-up Content sequencing, barrier analysis, next best action Outcomes are subjective operational labels
    Consent status Consent or opt-out indicator Whether a channel is permitted Channel eligibility and governance Varies by channel and jurisdiction
    Sample activity Channel, sample_drops Recorded sample units Sample utilization, follow-up planning Samples do not prove treatment initiation
    Digital engagement Channel, content, open, click, attendance Observable digital response Channel preference, engagement scoring, sequencing Engagement does not prove prescribing intent
    Field alignment Region, rep, accounts, HCPs, targets Assigned workload and coverage Capacity planning, workload audit, allocation Alignments change; they need effective dates

    Table 3A.6. Specialty-pharmacy case-summary fields and uses.

    Field group Important fields What it describes Later analytical uses Main caution
    Case identity sp_event_id, patient_id, product_ndc One synthetic referral case for one patient-product attempt Fulfillment pathway audit, cross-table trace This table is a case summary, not a log of every operational touch
    Referral timing referral_date, auth_decision_date, ship_date Key dated milestones in the case Delay analysis, approval timing, shipment lag Missing dates can reflect status, not data loss
    Authorization state auth_status, delivery_status Whether the case was approved, denied, pending, shipped, or abandoned Rejection analysis, support burden, abandonment Status combinations must be interpreted with the table grain in mind
    Patient-support detail copay_assistance, abandonment_reason, channel Affordability and operational context Support design, abandonment review, channel mix One field never captures the whole reason a case stalled
    Source linkage patient_id, product NDC Join back to claims, EHR, and reference tables Treatment-attempt tracing, case follow-up A shipped case is not the same thing as a paid pharmacy claim

    Table 3A.7. Medicare Part D prescriber-summary fields and uses.

    Field group Important fields Analytical use Main caution
    Prescriber NPI, name, specialty, state Provider matching, specialty segmentation, geography Synthetic HCP identifiers are not real NPIs
    Product Drug name, generic name, class Product grouping, competitive comparison Names need normalization across years and sources
    Utilization Total claims, beneficiaries, days supply Prescriber volume, reach, intensity Aggregates contain no patient sequences
    Cost Total drug cost Cost benchmarking, market description Cost is not company revenue or net price
    Time Calendar year Annual trends Annual files cannot support monthly timing
    Source Synthetic vs. official extract Provenance and interpretation The two must never be presented as equivalent
