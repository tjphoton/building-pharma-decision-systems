    Appendix 2A: Launch Team Role Reference

    This appendix defines the functions named in Chapter 2. Reporting lines and titles vary by company. The table groups roles by the work they support in the fictional Roventra launch.

    Field and Customer Support

    Role Primary responsibility Boundary
    Sales Representative Provides approved product information to HCPs and records customer interactions Clinical and coverage decisions remain with HCPs and payers
    District or Regional Sales Manager Sets field priorities, coaches representatives, and manages execution Works within the approved strategy and promotional rules
    Key Account Manager Coordinates work with health systems, integrated delivery networks, and large accounts Account committees retain their clinical protocol and purchasing decisions
    Field Reimbursement Manager Helps offices understand payer processes, prior authorization, and appeals The account completes clinical records and the payer determines coverage
    Medical Science Liaison Conducts scientific exchange and responds to unsolicited clinical questions Sits in Medical Affairs and remains separate from promotional direction
    Nurse Educator Provides approved education on treatment use and patient support The treating clinician retains clinical responsibility

    Marketing

    Function Primary responsibility Typical output
    Brand and HCP Marketing Develops positioning, messages, and HCP-facing programs Brand plan, campaign, approved field material
    Patient Marketing Provides patient-facing education where regulations allow Patient campaign, website, educational material
    Disease-State Education Builds unbranded awareness of the condition and its burden Disease education program
    Omnichannel and Digital Marketing Coordinates email, web, webinar, and other channel activity Channel plan and contact sequence
    Marketing Operations Manages content production and review workflow Approved content and production schedule

    Market Access and Reimbursement

    Function Primary responsibility Typical output
    Payer Account Director Leads account engagement with health plans and pharmacy-benefit managers Account plan and formulary discussion
    Pricing and Contracting Develops price, rebate, and contract terms Contract proposal and financial assessment
    Trade and Distribution Manages product movement through distributors and pharmacies Distribution agreement and channel plan
    Health Economics and Outcomes Research Develops economic and outcomes evidence for healthcare decision-makers Value evidence and economic analysis
    Policy and Government Affairs Tracks policy, legislation, and public coverage developments Policy assessment and response plan

    Patient Services

    Function Primary responsibility Boundary
    Benefits Investigation Confirms reported coverage requirements and patient cost information Provides current information without deciding coverage
    Prior-Authorization Support Helps offices identify forms and route required documentation The account supplies clinical records and the payer evaluates them
    Manufacturer Affordability Program Provides support to eligible patients under program rules Eligibility and use must follow legal and program requirements
    Independent Charitable Foundation Makes assistance decisions under its own governance Operates independently from the manufacturer
    Adherence and Persistence Support Provides approved reminders, education, and navigation The patient and treating clinician retain treatment decisions
    Specialty-Pharmacy Coordination Tracks referral, authorization, dispensing, and delivery steps The pharmacy applies its dispensing and operational requirements

    Operations, Analytics, and Leadership

    Function Primary responsibility Typical output
    Sales Operations Manages incentive compensation, territory alignment, and call planning Alignment, target list, call plan
    Commercial Operations Coordinates processes, platforms, and execution across the commercial organization Operating plan and performance process
    Data, CRM, and Commercial Technology Maintains customer data, interaction systems, and analytical platforms Governed data products and system records
    Decision Science and Analytics Turns data into evidence for choices about patients, accounts, channels, and resources Analysis, recommendation, measurement plan
    Forecasting Projects demand under explicit assumptions and scenarios Forecast and scenario range
    Market Research Studies customer attitudes, needs, and decision drivers Research report and insight
    Competitive Intelligence Tracks competitor, market, and access changes Competitive assessment
    Brand Lead Directs the brand strategy and plan Brand priorities and cross-functional decisions
    Commercial Lead or General Manager Holds responsibility for commercial performance and resource choices Business direction and resource allocation
    Launch Lead Coordinates readiness and execution across functions Integrated launch plan and readiness review

    Cross-Functional Partners

    Partner Primary responsibility Boundary
    Regulatory Reviews promotional claims and materials against the approved label and applicable requirements Commercial leadership sets business targets
    Legal and Compliance Interprets legal requirements and establishes compliant processes Business owners remain accountable for their decisions
    Finance Funds the plan and evaluates financial performance Clinical and coverage policy remain with the responsible external parties
    Supply Chain Plans inventory and product availability through the distribution network Payers retain approval decisions and pharmacies retain dispensing decisions
    Medical Affairs Leads scientific exchange and evidence work outside promotional direction Maintains separation from Commercial promotion
