    Chapter 2: The Commercialization Operating System

    FDA approval cleared Roventra for sale. The launch team now has to turn the approved label, payer plans, field options, supply plans, and patient-support programs into treatment for real patients. The first question is what the final label changed and where the existing launch plan must adjust.

    2.1 Decision Points Along the Patient Path

    Between a doctor's prescription and a patient's first dose sit several decisions and handoffs. A physician judges that the patient fits Roventra's approved indication and writes the order. From there, the prescription moves through checkpoints controlled by different parties: the account manages documentation, the payer evaluates coverage, the specialty pharmacy arranges fulfillment, and the patient decides whether to start therapy. The cross-functional launch team cannot control these external decisions, but it can remove friction, provide timely information, and measure where patients stop.

    Figure 2.1. The path along the patient prescription to treatment. Fictional market.

    Table 2.1 lists the five checkpoints and what prevents a prescription from advancing at each one.

    Table 2.1. The external decision-makers on the patient path.

    Party What they control What can delay treatment
    HCP Clinical fit and treatment selection The patient falls outside the approved indication, or the HCP selects Nexoral or Vexpro
    Account Documentation and workflow Missing chart notes, insurance information, test results, or required forms
    Payer Coverage eligibility and conditions Formulary restriction, prior authorization, step therapy, or new-to-market review
    Specialty pharmacy Coordination and fulfillment Benefit confirmation, affordability, dispensing, or delivery
    Patient Treatment initiation and persistence Affordability, concerns about treatment, side effects, or a decision not to start or continue

    The launch team supports each of these checkpoints. Section 2.2 shows which organizational functions support each stage.

    2.2 The Launch Organization Behind the Path

    Roventra's launch team brings together Commercial and partners from Medical Affairs, Legal and Compliance, Regulatory, Finance, and Supply Chain. Launch leadership (the brand lead, commercial lead, and launch lead) connects the work across these groups through brand strategy, commercial accountability, and cross-functional readiness. Table 2.2 maps those groups to the path. Detailed role definitions are in Appendix 2A.

    Table 2.2. The launch groups that support each stage of the patient path.

    Path stage External decision owner Launch groups that support the stage Support the team can provide
    Diagnosis and disease awareness HCP and patient Disease-state marketing (unbranded), Medical Affairs, Market Research Disease education and evidence about unmet need
    Treatment selection HCP Field Sales, Brand and Omnichannel Marketing, Medical Affairs Approved product education and scientific exchange through the appropriate channel
    Prescription and documentation Account Field Reimbursement, Key Account Management, Patient Services Current process guidance and help routing required documentation
    Coverage Payer Market Access, Pricing and Contracting, HEOR, Policy Formulary discussions, contract terms, and value evidence
    Fulfillment Specialty pharmacy Trade and Distribution, Patient Services, Supply Chain Benefit coordination, product availability, dispensing support, and delivery coordination
    Initiation and persistence Patient Patient Services, affordability programs, Nurse Educators, patient marketing Navigation, eligible affordability support, approved education, and adherence support
    All stages Launch leadership Commercial and Sales Operations, Decision Science and Analytics, Finance, Legal and Compliance, Regulatory Priorities, resources, measurement, approved processes, and governance
